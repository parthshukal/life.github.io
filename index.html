Q1) What does an improper fraction (4/2) mean, and why isn't it theoretical?

Before understanding the 'improper' , let's better brush up the 'proper'
Take 1/2 , Half indicates one of two pieces. If you make a full pizza, cut it into two pieces and take one, you have half a pizza.

1/2: The numerator indicates the number of pieces you have picked up and the denominator 'two' indicates the number of pieces that make up for a full pizza.

In the fraction presented above (4/2), the denominator is 2, i.e., 2 slices make up for a full pizza, or 1 slice equals half a pizza. The numerator is 4, indicating you have picked up 4 slices like these. This is something you can do! Improper fractions do exist, and it means you have had two full pizzas. 4/2 = 2.

Q2) Solving questions mentally: 1/2 + 12/25: is it less than 1 or more than 1?

Before understanding the 'improper' , let's better brush up the 'proper'
Adding two halves make up a full pizza. But in the above question, the first portion is half, but the second portion is less than half. You make 25 pieces; the exact half is 12.5. If you just take 12 pieces, it is less than half!

Hence, adding half (1/2) and less than half (12/25) shall be less than 1.

Q3) Why does adding zeroes after decimal points not affect the value?

Before understanding the 'improper' , let's better brush up the 'proper'
To understand this, think: what does 6.5 mean? It means making 6 full parts and then another half part.

The number of places after the decimal is 1. This means, after the 6 pieces you’ve taken, you make 10 parts of the 7th piece and take 5 parts out of them. You are effectively taking half of the 7th piece.

Now we will understand as to 6.5 = 6.50 The number of places after the decimal in 6.50 is 2. This means you make 100 parts of the 7th piece and take 50 parts out of them. You again end up taking half.

Adding zeros after the decimals mean that you simply make more parts, and you take more parts out of them, but the answer doesn't change.
